NAEP Score Changes from 2019 to 2024 at Key Points Along the Achievement Spectrum

Change-by-Baseline Plot: 2019 NAEP baseline score (x-axis) vs. 2024 minus 2019 change (y-axis), across percentiles (10/25/50/75/90) by state.

What This Dashboard Shows

As featured in the ETS Research Institute Blog, this dashboard helps visualize the relationship between pre-COVID NAEP scores (2019) and score changes since the pandemic (2024 scores minus 2019 scores) for different locales (states, districts, and geographic regions). Check out the blog for more details.

For a particular grade-subject combination, each locale has a line that connects the score changes for students at low (10th and 25th percentiles), moderate (50th percentile), and high (75th and 90th percentiles) points of the student achievement spectrum. These lines illustrate how changes from pre to post pandemic varied across the achievement distribution within each state:
  • Flatter lines indicate more comparable score changes along the distribution.
  • Upward sloping lines suggest larger drops at the low end of the spectrum or a widening of the achievement disparity between lower- and higher-scoring students from 2019 to 2024.
  • An asterisk (*) next to the state’s name in the legend indicates a significantly (p < 0.05) larger drop at the 10th percentile than the 90th percentile.
  • A black border around a plotting symbol indicates a significant change (p < 0.05) in the percentile score from 2019 to 2024.
  • Score changes at percentile points can be influenced by changes in student makeup over time. Check out your state's data in the NAEP Data Explorer.
  • The horizontal line at zero helps distinguish cases with score increases (above the zero line) from those with score decreases (below the zero line).
