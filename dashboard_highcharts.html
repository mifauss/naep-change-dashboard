Tracking Cohort Change in NAEP Scores across the Achievement Spectrum

Change-by-Baseline Plot: 2019 NAEP baseline score (x-axis) vs. 2024 minus 2019 change (y-axis), across percentiles (10/25/50/75/90) by state.

What This Dashboard Shows

As featured in the ETS Research Institute Blog [here], this dashboard helps visualize the relationship between pre-COVID NAEP scores (2019) and score changes since the pandemic (2024 scores minus 2019 scores) for states and jurisdictions (e.g., Puerto Rico). Check out the blog for more details.

For a particular grade-subject combination, each state and jurisdiction has a line that connects the score changes for students at low (10th and 25th percentiles), moderate (50th percentile), and high (75th and 90th percentiles) points of the student achievement spectrum. These lines illustrate how changes from pre to post pandemic varied across the achievement distribution within each state:
  • Flatter lines indicate more comparable score changes along the distribution.
  • Upward sloping lines suggest larger drops at the low end of the spectrum or a widening of the achievement disparity between lower- and higher-scoring students from 2019 to 2024.
  • An asterisk (*) next to the state’s name in the legend indicates a significantly (p < 0.05) larger drop at the 10th percentile than the 90th percentile.
  • The horizontal line at zero helps distinguish cases with score increases (above the zero line) from those with score decreases (below the zero line).

How to use
Use the controls above to choose subject and grade. In “Selected States” mode, pick specific states. In the chart, click legend entries to show/hide a state.
